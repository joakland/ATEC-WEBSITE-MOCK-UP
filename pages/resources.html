Your Resume

A great resume is crucial for showcasing your skills and qualifications. Update it for each application.

Does your resume…

  • Include a specific job objective?
  • Include full contact info: name, address, phone or message number, and email?
  • Summarize the skills and qualifications needed for the job (matching keywords from the posting)?
  • Show dates of employment and/or volunteer experience?
  • Look professional — double-check spelling and grammar.

Cover Letters

A cover letter introduces you to an employer and accompanies your job application or resume. It highlights your qualifications in detail and connects your skills and experience to the specific job you're applying for.

5 Tips for a Successful Interview

1

Know yourself

Be self-aware and confident. List your strengths, skills, achievements, and hobbies.

2

Research the job

Understand what the company is looking for. Analyze job postings, check the careers page, and reach out to current employees to learn about the desired skills and values.

3

Match yourself to the job

Go through the job description carefully. For each requirement, show why you're a better fit than other candidates.

4

Practice interviews

Ask friends or family for mock interviews, review common questions, and practice body language and your handshake.

5

Dress appropriately

Dress one level above the expected attire — e.g., "business casual" for a casual workplace.

Items to avoid: Open-toed shoes, heavy makeup, revealing/clingy clothing, short skirts, visible piercings, large tattoos, platform shoes, large earrings, wrinkled clothing, or hair in your face.

Follow Up

There are typically three follow-up emails after an interview:

  1. A thank-you note to your interviewers immediately after the interview.
  2. A second follow-up if you haven't heard back.
  3. A "check-in" email to stay in touch for networking purposes.

In the best case, you only need to send one — a note thanking your interviewers and expressing your enthusiasm for the role.
